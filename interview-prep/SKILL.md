## nSARl Framework

The primary response framework for behavioral questions:

```
n - Nugget    → Hook at beginning (one sentence summary of outcome)
S - Situation → Context (2-3 sentences max, just enough to understand)
A - Action    → What YOU did (this is 60% of your answer)
R - Result    → Quantified outcomes (ALWAYS include numbers)
l - Lessons   → What you learned (tie it forward)
```

### Example Structure

```
"The time I [outcome/hook]"                           ← Nugget

"I was working at [company] as [role].                ← Situation
 We faced [specific challenge] which meant [stakes]."

"First, I [action 1].                                 ← Actions
 Then I [action 2].
 The key insight was [action 3].
 I also [action 4]."

"As a result, we achieved [metric 1], [metric 2].     ← Result
 The team went from [before] to [after]."

"What I learned was [lesson].                         ← Lessons
 I now apply this by [current behavior]."
```

## TMAY (Tell Me About Yourself)

Write your 90-second intro:

```
[Opening - who you are now]

[Career arc - 2-3 sentences covering your journey]

[Why this role/company - connect your experience to their needs]

[Closing - what you're excited about]
```

### Role Variations

- **For Product roles**: Emphasize customer insight, shipping, metrics
- **For GM roles**: Emphasize P&L, team building, cross-functional
- **For Leadership roles**: Emphasize vision, culture, scaling

## Story Bank

### Template for Each Story

```markdown
### [Story Name]

**Nugget**: "The time I [outcome]"

**Situation**:
- [Context about role/company]
- [The specific challenge]
- [Why it mattered/stakes]

**Actions**:
1. [First thing you did]
2. [Second thing you did]
3. [Key insight or pivot]
4. [How you executed]

**Result**:
- [Quantified outcome 1]
- [Quantified outcome 2]
- [Before → After if applicable]

**Lessons**:
- [What you learned]
- [How you apply it now]

**Best for prompts**:
- "Tell me about a time you..."
- "How do you handle..."
```

## Behavioral Question Bank

### Leadership
- Tell me about a time you led a team through ambiguity
- Describe a situation where you had to make a tough call without full information
- How have you built and scaled a team?

### Influence
- Tell me about influencing without direct authority
- Describe working with a difficult stakeholder
- How do you get buy-in for unpopular decisions?

### Product
- Walk me through launching a product from 0 to 1
- How do you prioritize competing features?
- Describe a product decision that didn't work out

### Data & Decisions
- How do you make decisions when data is limited?
- Tell me about using data to change strategy
- Describe balancing intuition vs data

### Conflict & Growth
- Tell me about a significant failure and what you learned
- Describe a time you received difficult feedback
- How do you handle disagreements with leadership?

### Customer Focus
- Tell me about a time you deeply understood customer needs
- How do you balance customer requests vs vision?
- Describe advocating for the customer against business pressure

## Company-Specific Frameworks

### Google
- **Focus on**: Scale, data-driven decisions, "Googleyness"
- **Emphasize**: Collaboration, impact measurement, user focus
- **Question style**: "Tell me about a time..." (behavioral heavy)

### Meta
- **Focus on**: Move fast, impact, bold bets
- **Emphasize**: Shipping velocity, metrics, learning from failure
- **Question style**: Mixed behavioral + product sense

### Amazon
- **Focus on**: Leadership Principles (Customer Obsession, Bias for Action, etc.)
- **Emphasize**: Data, ownership, disagree and commit
- **Question style**: STAR format expected

### Healthcare/Regulated
- **Focus on**: Compliance, patient safety, trust
- **Emphasize**: Stakeholder management, evidence-based decisions
- **Question style**: Often scenario-based

## Pre-Interview Checklist

### Day Before
- [ ] Research company recent news (last 30 days)
- [ ] Review job posting, highlight key requirements
- [ ] Prepare 3 questions for each interviewer
- [ ] Review your top 5 stories
- [ ] Practice TMAY out loud

### Morning Of
- [ ] Review company values/principles
- [ ] Re-read job description
- [ ] Quick story refresh (Quick Recall table)
- [ ] Check interviewer LinkedIn profiles
- [ ] Prepare tech (camera, mic, quiet space)

### 2 Minutes Before
- [ ] Water nearby
- [ ] TMAY ready
- [ ] 3 stories top of mind
- [ ] 2 questions ready
- [ ] Smile, breathe

## Do/Don't

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Use "I" not "we" for your actions | Be vague about your role |
| Include numbers in every result | Give results without metrics |
| Keep situation brief (20% of answer) | Spend too long on context |
| Show lessons learned | Just end with the result |
| Tailor stories to the company | Use the same exact story everywhere |
| Admit what you'd do differently | Be defensive about failures |

## Questions to Ask

### For Hiring Manager
- What does success look like in 90 days?
- What's the biggest challenge the team is facing?
- How do you measure performance for this role?

### For Skip Level
- What's the team's reputation in the org?
- How does this team fit into company strategy?
- What do you wish this team did more of?

### For Peers
- What's collaboration like across teams?
- What's something you wish you knew before joining?
- What's the decision-making process like?

### For Reports (if applicable)
- What does your ideal manager do?
- What's working well on the team? What isn't?
- How do you prefer to receive feedback?

## Product Frameworks

### CIRCLES (Product Design)
- **C**omprehend situation
- **I**dentify customer
- **R**eport customer needs
- **C**ut through prioritization
- **L**ist solutions
- **E**valuate tradeoffs
- **S**ummarize recommendation

### GAME (Estimation)
- **G**ather info / clarify
- **A**ssume and articulate
- **M**ath it out
- **E**valuate / sanity check

### AARM (Strategy)
- **A**nalyze market
- **A**ssess competitors
- **R**ecommend strategy
- **M**etrics to track
