## Investor Psychology: Confidence vs Rigor

### **Too Declarative (Current Slide 8):**
❌ Risk: "They're assuming pricing without proof"
❌ Risk: "What if labs won't pay $150-400?"
❌ Risk: "Feels overconfident for pre-revenue"

### **Too Tentative (Pure Experimental Framing):**
❌ Risk: "They don't know their business model yet"
❌ Risk: "Too academic, not execution-focused"
❌ Risk: "Seed investors want proven model, not experiments"

### **Sweet Spot: "Informed Thesis with Systematic Validation"**
✅ Confidence: "Here's our model based on LOIs and market research"
✅ Rigor: "We're systematically testing key assumptions before scaling"
✅ Traction: "Protean LOI validates lab pricing; experiments de-risk the rest"

**Message:**
> "We're not guessing. We have a model informed by pilot learnings and LOI negotiations. Now we're running experiments to prove what works before we scale."
