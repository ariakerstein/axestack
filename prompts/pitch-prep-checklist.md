# Pitch Deck Preparation Checklist

## Metrics & Data to Prepare

Use this checklist to ensure you have all necessary data points ready before creating or updating pitch decks.

### Customer Economics

- [ ] **Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)**
  - Cost per customer acquired
  - Breakdown by channel (if available)
  - CAC trend over time

- [ ] **Lifetime Value (LTV)**
  - Average revenue per customer
  - Customer retention rate
  - LTV calculation methodology

- [ ] **LTV:CAC Ratio**
  - Target: 3:1 or higher for healthy unit economics
  - Show trajectory if not yet at target

### Conversion Funnel

- [ ] **Top of Funnel**
  - Website visitors / Landing page views
  - Signup conversion rate

- [ ] **Middle of Funnel**
  - Documents uploaded
  - Gap analysis completed
  - Active users (DAU/MAU)

- [ ] **Bottom of Funnel**
  - Actions taken (referrals clicked, appointments scheduled)
  - Conversion rate from signup → action
  - Time to first value

### Revenue Metrics

- [ ] **Revenue Per Customer**
  - Average revenue per user (ARPU)
  - Breakdown by revenue stream (subscription, referral fees, etc.)

- [ ] **Revenue Per Action**
  - Revenue per referral
  - Revenue per completed journey
  - Commission rates by partner type

- [ ] **Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR)**
  - Current MRR
  - MRR growth rate (month-over-month)
  - Breakdown: new, expansion, churn

### Growth Projections

#### 12-Month Milestones
- [ ] Customer count
- [ ] Revenue target
- [ ] Key product features to ship
- [ ] Team size
- [ ] Burn rate and runway

#### 24-Month Milestones
- [ ] Customer count
- [ ] Revenue target
- [ ] Market expansion (new cancer types, geographies)
- [ ] Strategic partnerships
- [ ] Team size

#### 36-Month Milestones
- [ ] Customer count
- [ ] Revenue target
- [ ] Platform capabilities
- [ ] Path to profitability or next funding round

### Unit Economics Assumptions

- [ ] **Test Referral Fees**
  - Average referral value by test type
  - Conversion rate (referral → completed test)
  - Partner commission structure

- [ ] **Subscription ARPU**
  - Monthly subscription tiers
  - Expected tier distribution
  - Churn assumptions

- [ ] **Partner Economics**
  - Revenue share percentages
  - Payment terms
  - Volume commitments

### Market Size

- [ ] **TAM (Total Addressable Market)**
  - Maximum market size if 100% penetration
  - Credible source citation
  - Calculation methodology

- [ ] **SAM (Serviceable Addressable Market)**
  - Portion of TAM you can realistically reach
  - Geographic/demographic constraints
  - Product limitations

- [ ] **SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market)**
  - Realistic market share in 3-5 years
  - Based on competition and go-to-market strategy
  - Conservative estimate

### Supporting Data Points

- [ ] **Clinical Data**
  - Care coordination statistics (e.g., "80% happens outside clinic")
  - Diagnostic error rates
  - Trial enrollment challenges
  - Cost of care fragmentation

- [ ] **Competitive Landscape**
  - Direct competitors and their funding
  - Indirect competitors (status quo)
  - Your differentiation (moat)

- [ ] **User Testimonials**
  - 2-3 compelling quotes
  - Quantified outcomes when possible
  - Permission to use publicly

- [ ] **Team Credentials**
  - Prior company scale (users, revenue)
  - Relevant domain expertise
  - Notable advisors or investors

## Pre-Pitch Preparation

### For Investor Meetings

- [ ] Know your ask (amount, valuation, terms)
- [ ] Prepared for common objections
- [ ] 3-5 questions to ask investors
- [ ] 1-page executive summary (PDF)
- [ ] Financial model ready to share
- [ ] Data room organized (if needed)

### For Demo Day / Competition

- [ ] Timed your pitch (stay under limit)
- [ ] Practiced Q&A with team
- [ ] Backup plan if tech fails
- [ ] Printable one-pager for judges
- [ ] Contact info clearly visible

### For Partner / BD Pitches

- [ ] Customized value prop for their business
- [ ] Integration effort estimates
- [ ] Pricing/revenue share proposal
- [ ] Case studies or pilot results
- [ ] Implementation timeline

---

**Last Updated**: October 31, 2024

**Note**: Not all metrics will be available for early-stage startups. Be honest about what you have vs. what are projections/assumptions. Investors appreciate transparency.
