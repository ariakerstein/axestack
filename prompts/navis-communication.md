# Navis Communication Guide: From Confusion to Clarity

**Objective:** This guide provides a comprehensive framework for all Navis communications. It ensures a consistent, empathetic, and empowering voice across all platforms, speaking directly to the needs of cancer patients, caregivers, and partners. The goal is to build trust, drive action, and establish Navis as the definitive leader in the Patient Relationship Management (PRM) category.

This document synthesizes insights from Navis's core strategy, the successful peer-support language of AnCan, and best practices in health tech communication.

---

## Part 1: The Navis Voice & Soul

### 1.1 Our Core Principles

These principles are the foundation of every piece of communication.

- **Patient Trust & Control First, Always:** We are guardians of patient trust. Our language is transparent, secure, and always emphasizes patient ownership of their data and decisions. We never promise cures or give medical advice.
- **From Overwhelm to Action:** Our primary function is to cut through the "choppy ocean of options." Every communication should lead the user from a state of confusion to a clear, manageable next step.
- **Empathy Backed by Evidence:** We speak with the warmth of a survivor who has "been there" but provide the competence of a platform built on medical-grade AI and verified information. We are compassionate _and_ credible.
- **Clarity is Kindness:** We use plain, simple language, avoiding jargon and corporate-speak. We translate complex medical concepts into understandable terms, empowering patients to "speak doctor."
- **Two-Sided Value, One Mission:** We serve both patients and partners, but our north star is always the patient's well-being. Partner messaging focuses on the ROI of connecting with qualified, empowered patients.

### 1.3 Forbidden Language: What We AVOID

To protect trust and maintain clarity, we consciously avoid certain words and phrases.

| ✗ AVOID THIS                                      | ✓ USE THIS INSTEAD                                              | Why?                                                                                    |
| :------------------------------------------------ | :-------------------------------------------------------------- | :-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| "Cure," "Guarantee," "Promise"                    | "Path," "Option," "Opportunity," "Plan"                         | Medical and ethical integrity. We manage possibilities, not certainties.                |
| "Empowerment," "Patient-centric"                  | "Be your own best advocate," "Put you in control"               | Show, don't tell. Use active, benefit-driven language instead of buzzwords.             |
| "Revolutionary," "Disruptive"                     | "A new way," "The layer that's been missing"                    | Hype erodes trust. Patients are skeptical. Explain the change, don't just label it.     |
| "Platform," "Leverage," "Synergy"                 | "Toolset," "Guide," "Use," "Connect"                            | Jargon creates distance. Use simple, human words.                                       |
| "Journey" (when overused)                         | "Path," "Stage," "Process," "Your experience"                   | "Journey" has become a cliché. Be more specific to the context.                         |
| "This is not medical advice..." (long disclaimer) | "We guide you to verified experts; you and your doctor decide." | State it simply and positively. Frame it as a feature (guidance), not just a liability. |

## Part 2: Speaking to The Patient Soul (B2C)

This section is built on the deep understanding of patient archetypes, drawing heavily from the effective, peer-tested language of AnCan.

### 2.1 Patient Archetype Messaging

#### Archetype 1: The Newly Diagnosed Overwhelmed (Week 0-4)

- **Their Core Feeling:** Fear, shock, isolation. "I'm drowning in a choppy ocean of options."
- **Their Urgent Need:** Orientation and the next _one_ right thing to do.
- **Navis Promise:** We are your **life vest**. We turn chaos into a clear, 3-step plan.
- **Key Phrases:**
  - "You just heard the word 'cancer.' Take a breath. You're in the right place."
  - "From diagnosis shock to your first clear steps."
  - "We'll help you find the right questions to ask before you even think of them."
  - "Connect with verified second opinion centers without chasing faxes."

#### Archetype 2: The Treatment-Stage Warrior (Months 3-18)

- **Their Core Feeling:** Fatigue, frustration, determination. "No one told me about..."
- **Their Urgent Need:** Practical hacks for side effects and quality of life.
- **Navis Promise:** We are your **playbook**. We connect you to resources for managing the day-to-day fight.
- **Key Phrases:**
  - "Managing treatment is more than just appointments. It's about your life."
  - "Find verified support for side effects your doctor might call 'normal'."
  - "Connect with resources for nutrition, physical therapy, and mental health—all vetted for cancer patients."
  - "Track your progress in one place, so you can see how far you've come."

#### Archetype 3: The Recurrent/Advanced Fighter (Year 1+)

- **Their Core Feeling:** Urgency, grief, warrior-like determination. "I wish I'd found this earlier."
- **Their Urgent Need:** Cutting-edge options and a way to fight smarter.
- **Navis Promise:** We are your **Ranger training**. We give you the advanced tools to fight toe-to-toe with your disease.
- **Key Phrases:**
  - "When standard options are not enough, it's time to find what's next."
  - "Our AI finds clinical trials you may be eligible for based on your specific diagnosis."
  - "Access advanced testing like liquid biopsies that can guide your next treatment."
  - "You've become an expert in your own cancer. We provide the tools to match your expertise."

## Part 3: Speaking to Partners & Investors (B2B)

This section focuses on communicating the value of the PRM category and the tangible ROI Navis delivers.

### 3.1 Core Messaging: Creating the "Patient Relationship Management" Category

**The Elevator Pitch:** "Healthcare has EHRs for clinical records and CRMs for sales, but nothing for the actual patient relationship. Navis created the **Patient Relationship Management (PRM)** category—the engagement layer that guides patients between visits and delivers qualified, attributable value back to our partners."

### 3.2 Value Proposition by Partner Type

#### For Labs & Diagnostic Companies:

- **Their Pain:** Awareness gap and no attribution. "We have a groundbreaking test, but the right patients never hear about it."
- **Navis Promise:** **We deliver qualified patients with clear ROI.**
- **Key Messages:**
  - "Stop marketing into the void. Reach patients the moment our AI identifies your test is relevant."
  - "Our attribution dashboard proves your ROI—no more guessing."
  - "Educate patients on _why_ they need your test before they even talk to their doctor."

#### For Academic Centers & Second Opinion Programs:

- **Their Pain:** Inefficient intake and lack of differentiation. "Patients arrive with incomplete records, and we look just like every other major hospital online."
- **Navis Promise:** **Receive prepared patients, ready for consultation.**
- **Key Messages:**
  - "Patients arrive with their complete medical history organized in their Navis Vault. Go from intake to consult in days, not weeks."
  - "Stand out from the noise. We match you with patients specifically seeking your center's expertise."
  - "Get analytics on your patient funnel, from discovery to appointment."

#### For Clinical Trial Sponsors:

- **Their Pain:** Recruitment is the #1 bottleneck. "We spend $30K per enrolled patient and still miss our targets."
- **Navis Promise:** **Accelerate recruitment with AI-matched, eligible patients.**
- **Key Messages:**
  - "Our medical AI pre-screens patients, matching your trial's inclusion/exclusion criteria to their records."
  - "Reduce screen failure rates by connecting with informed patients who are actively seeking trials."
  - "Fill your trials faster and bring life-saving therapies to market sooner."
