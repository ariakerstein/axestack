- **One-liner (plain English)**

  - Navis is a patient-friendly marketplace and toolset that turns cancer care confusion into clear next steps, connecting people to verified testing, second opinions, trials, providers, and advocacy—plus a secure place to store/share records.

- **The core problem they solve**

  - Patients face high-stakes choices with missing info and overwhelm (e.g., 72% don’t know targeted testing is an option; 40% can’t name their disease; diagnostic errors harm ~795k Americans/year).

- **What Navis does (product in 3 beats)**

  - **Phase-based guidance:** step-by-step options that explain _why_ each appears for you now.
  - **Verified matches:** one place to find trustworthy labs, second-opinion centers, providers, clinical trials, advocacy, and lifestyle support.
  - **Patient Vault + consent:** organize records and share them with family, advocates, and partners—on your terms. Every match improves data quality and trust.

- **How it works (simple flow)**

  - Upload/collect records → medical-grade contextual AI adds the right context → you see vetted next actions → you connect to verified partners → you can keep collaborating via shared vault and permissions.

- **Who it serves (two-sided)**

  - **Patients & families:** clarity, control, faster second opinions/testing, easier trial matching, and organized sharing.
  - **Partners (labs, centers, advocacy, etc.):** qualified patients, attribution/ROI analytics, and white-label “Powered by Navis” portals to manage relationships long-term.

- **What Navis is _not_**

  - Not a hospital or doctor; it doesn’t replace medical care—it guides and connects you to it.
  - Not a generic directory, ad-only site, or social forum; it’s oncology-specific, verified, and phase-aware with measurable ROI for partners.
  - Not an EHR or a sales CRM; it creates a _new_ layer—**Patient Relationship Management (PRM)**—focused on engagement and new value streams between patients and service orgs.

- **Values & non-negotiables (visible in product choices)**

  - **Patient trust & control:** “Chief Trust Officer” on the founding team; verified partners; consented sharing; clear attribution.
  - **Evidence and transparency:** medical-grade context and verified listings over “noisy” generic content.
  - **Outcome focus:** convert patient intent into real actions for patients and measurable ROI for partners.

- **Business model (how they make money)**

  - **Partners:**

    - Verified partner (listed after verification) — **$0**.
    - Active listing — **$299/mo**.
    - Featured/Sponsored — **$999/mo** + analytics.

  - **Patients (optional):** Navis Plus membership **$19–59/mo** for premium features (AI credits, storage, alerts, etc.).
  - **White-label portals:** “Powered-by-Navis” setups (**$3–10k** setup + **$299–999/mo**) to turn partners into patient on-ramps.

- **Go-to-market (how they grow)**

  - **Adopt → Monetize → Expand:** start free with advocacy orgs to drive zero-CAC patient adoption; sell SaaS to labs using that funnel as proof; later expand to employers.
  - **Build → Prove → Scale:** yrs 1–2 onboard 200 partners + 5 lab pilots and 20k patients; yrs 3–5 scale to 600–2,000 partners and 250k+ patients; ARR target ~$25M+ at 80–85% GM.

- **Market & timing (why now)**

  - Massive, fragmented space with no trusted discovery layer; near-term U.S. SOM ~$500M; SAM ~$10–15B; TAM ~$100B+.
  - Trends: healthcare consumerization, cancer becoming chronic, testing exploding (e.g., liquid biopsy growth), record portability mandates (TEFCA).

- **Tech/approach**

  - **Contextual AI** to translate records and surface phase-specific choices.
  - **PRM category creation:** fills the gap between EHR (clinical/billing) and CRM (sales) for ongoing patient relationships.
  - **Trust signals:** verified patients, consented data-sharing, and ROI analytics that give partners confidence.

- **Early proof / signals**

  - MVP shipped; ongoing patient interviews; >60% intent to adopt reported; weekly webinars; letters of intent across advocacy, academic referrals, and precision testing; strong patient quotes.
