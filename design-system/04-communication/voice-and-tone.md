# Voice & Tone

## Who We Are

A calm, knowledgeable guide. Not a chatbot. Not a doctor. Not a salesperson.

We speak like a trusted friend who happens to know a lot about cancer care — someone who explains things clearly, never talks down, and always has a next step ready.

---

## Voice Attributes

| Attribute | What It Sounds Like | What It Doesn't Sound Like |
|-----------|--------------------|-----------------------------|
| **Empathetic** | "This is scary. Here's what you can do." | "Sorry for your diagnosis. We hope..." |
| **Clear** | "3 tests may be missing" | "Our analysis suggests potential gaps" |
| **Authoritative** | "NCCN guidelines recommend..." | "You might want to maybe consider..." |
| **Action-oriented** | "Ask your oncologist about..." | "It's important to be aware of..." |

---

## Benefits, Not Features

Every piece of copy frames what the USER gets, not what the PRODUCT does.

| Feature Copy (DON'T) | Benefit Copy (DO) |
|----------------------|-------------------|
| Upload records | Find gaps in your care |
| NCCN-recommended tests | See what tests you may need |
| AI-powered analysis | Get answers in 24 hours |
| Questions for your doctor | Know exactly what to ask |
| Clinical trial matching | Find trials near you |
| Care circle management | Keep your family in the loop |

## Forbidden Language

| Never Say | Why | Say Instead |
|-----------|-----|-------------|
| "Loved one" (repeated / in-app) | Generic when you already know their name | Their actual name or "them" — see "Loved One" Rule below |
| "The patient" | Dehumanizing | Their name or "you" |
| "Fight/battle/warrior" | Not everyone identifies with combat metaphors | "Your care journey" |
| "We understand how you feel" | Generic empathy falls flat | "We've been there — here's what helped us" (founded by survivors, so be specific) |
| "Don't worry" | Dismissive of valid fear | "Here's what you can do" |
| "Just" | Minimizes difficulty | Remove the word entirely |
| "Click here" | Non-descriptive | Describe the action: "Get your checklist" |
| "Learn more" | Vague, passive | Specific: "See which tests you need" |
| "Submit" | Cold, form-like | "Get My Results" / "Continue" |
| "AI" (prominent) | Creates distance | "Expert-backed" or just don't mention it |
| "Comprehensive/robust" | Marketing fluff | Be specific about what's included |

---

## The "Loved One" Rule

"Loved one" is how caregivers naturally describe the person they're caring for — it's identity language, not a euphemism. Banning it entirely forces clinical alternatives ("the person you care for") that create distance from our caregiver audience. The real problem is lazy repetition and using it as a substitute for specificity.

**Guideline: prefer the patient's name when you have it. Use "loved one" sparingly in contexts where you don't.**

| Context | Use "loved one"? | Why |
|---------|------------------|-----|
| Ads / SEO / first-visit copy | Yes | Matches caregiver search intent, no name available yet |
| Caregiver onboarding (pre-profile) | Yes, sparingly | Identity-affirming, builds trust before you have a name |
| Caregiver onboarding (post-name) | No — use their name | Now you have specificity, use it |
| In-app after profile exists | No — use their name | You know who they're caring for |
| Patient-facing copy | Never | Patients don't refer to themselves this way |
| Overused on the same page | No | If it starts feeling like a refrain, you've overdone it |

**Don't lean on it.** A couple of natural uses on a page is fine. But if it starts feeling like a refrain, vary your language — "them," "they," the patient's name, or a natural alternative that fits the tone (e.g., "someone you care about," "the person you're supporting," "your family member"). Use your judgment to pick what reads best in context.

---

## Patient Name References

- Use their actual name when available: "Sarah's care plan"
- When name unknown: "your" (speaking to patient) or "them" (speaking to caregiver)
- For caregiver view: "[Patient name]'s records" not "Your loved one's records"

## Tone by Context

| Context | Tone | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| **Error/failure** | Direct, solution-focused | "Upload failed. Try a smaller file or different format." |
| **Success** | Warm, brief | "Record uploaded. We'll analyze it shortly." |
| **Onboarding** | Encouraging, simple | "Let's start with your cancer type." |
| **Medical content** | Clear, factual | "NCCN recommends BRCA testing for all breast cancer patients." |
| **Empty state** | Helpful, action-oriented | "No records yet. Upload your first to find care gaps." |
| **Payment** | Honest, anchored | "What costs $4,000 elsewhere — $199 here." |
