# Copy Patterns

## Rule #1: Frontload

People scan, they don't read. The first 2-3 words of every element must carry the meaning.

| Bad (buried lead) | Good (frontloaded) |
|-------------------|--------------------|
| "Our platform helps you find missing tests" | "Missing tests? Find them in 2 minutes" |
| "Click here to get started with your care" | "Get your checklist" |
| "We've analyzed your records and found..." | "3 gaps found in your care plan" |
| "Based on NCCN guidelines, you may need..." | "2 tests recommended for your cancer type" |
| "Thank you for uploading your document" | "Record uploaded. Analyzing now." |

## Headline Formulas

### Problem → Promise
"[Scary stat/problem]. [What we do about it]."
- "84% of patients miss critical tests. See what YOU might be missing."
- "Second opinions cost $4,000. Yours is $49."

### Stat → Question
"[Specific number]. [Question that creates curiosity]."
- "1 in 3 patients had treatment changes after a second opinion. Will you?"
- "35% of second opinions lead to treatment changes. What about yours?"

### Before → After
"[Current painful state] → [Better state with us]."
- "Waiting 2 weeks for answers → Get yours in 24 hours"
- "Drowning in medical jargon → Plain language you understand"

### Identity → Action
"[Who you are] → [What you do]."
- "You're taking control of your care. Start here."
- "Caregivers who don't miss anything use this."

## CTA Text Patterns

### Primary CTAs (Start with strong verb)
- "Get Your Free Checklist"
- "See What Tests You Need"
- "Find Clinical Trials Near You"
- "Start Your Second Look"
- "Upload Your Records"
- "Prep for Your Appointment"

### Secondary CTAs (Softer action)
- "View details"
- "Share with your doctor"
- "Tell us more"
- "Skip for now"

### Never
- "Submit" (cold)
- "Click here" (generic)
- "Learn more" (passive — be specific about what they'll learn)
- "Sign up" as primary (delay until after value)
- "Buy now" (transactional — frame around what they GET)

## Card Copy Pattern

```
[Benefit headline — 5-8 words]
[One sentence: what they get, specifically]
[CTA button]
```

### Examples

```
Find missing tests
See which NCCN-recommended tests your plan may be missing.
[Get Your Checklist]
```

```
Prep for your next appointment
5 questions to ask your oncologist, specific to your diagnosis.
[Start Prep]
```

```
Match clinical trials
Trials within 50 miles that match your cancer type and biomarkers.
[Find Trials]
```

## Error & Empty State Copy

### Errors (Direct, solution-focused)
- "Upload failed. Try a smaller file (max 25MB) or PDF format."
- "Connection lost. Your data is saved — try again."
- "Something went wrong. [Try again] or [contact support]."

### Empty States (Action-oriented)
- "No records yet. Upload your first to find care gaps."
- "No trials matched. Update your profile for better matches."
- "No questions asked. Try: 'What tests do I need for [cancer type]?'"

Every empty state MUST have a CTA. Never a dead end.

## Numbers & Specificity

Specific > vague. Always.

| Vague | Specific |
|-------|---------|
| "Many patients" | "1 in 3 patients" |
| "Saves time" | "Get answers in 24 hours" |
| "Affordable" | "$49" |
| "Top doctors" | "Board-certified oncologists from NCI-designated centers" |
| "Helps you" | "Finds 3-5 tests your plan may be missing" |

---

## Inverted Pyramid

Structure all content with most important information first:

```
1. What they get (benefit/outcome)
2. How it works (1-2 sentences)
3. Why it's credible (trust signal)
4. Details (for those who want them — often behind "Show more")
```

Never start with "how it works" or background context. Start with the payoff.

## Per-Archetype Copy Quick Reference

| Profile | Headline Style | CTA Style | Tone |
|---------|---------------|-----------|------|
| **Newly Diagnosed** | Reassuring + specific | "Get your [specific thing]" | Calm, grounding |
| **Treatment-Stage** | Practical + time-bounded | "Prep for [specific event]" | Efficient, empowering |
| **Recurrent/Advanced** | Possibility + novel | "Find [new options]" | Hopeful, thorough |
| **Caregiver** | Action + sharing | "Share with [name/doctor]" | Supportive, organized |
